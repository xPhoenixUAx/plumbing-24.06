Below The Surface

Diagnostics and repair planning for sewer line issues.

Sewer problems can affect the whole property. Warning signs include multiple fixtures backing up, sewage odors, wet spots in the yard, gurgling toilets, or drains that slow down together. Sewer requests should be approached with careful diagnosis before any invasive work is approved.

Camera inspection helps identify roots, offsets, bellies, breaks, heavy buildup, or foreign objects. When repair is needed, clear location information can make a provider estimate more accurate and reduce unnecessary disruption.

What a provider may review

  • Symptom review across toilets, tubs, floor drains, and cleanouts.
  • Accessible cleanout inspection and line clearing recommendations.
  • Sewer camera inspection when conditions allow.
  • Blockage location and likely-cause explanation.
  • Repair planning for damaged, offset, or collapsed sections.
  • Preventive guidance for roots, grease, wipes, and recurring backups.
  • Documentation for property owners or managers.

When camera inspection helps

If a line clogs repeatedly, clearing alone may not solve the reason. A camera view can show whether the issue is buildup, root intrusion, slope, or pipe damage, which changes the right repair path.

Worksite care

Sewer work can be disruptive, so ask providers about clean access, communication, repair options, immediate mitigation, and planned follow-up before hiring.

Warning Signs

  • Multiple backups
  • Sewer odor
  • Gurgling fixtures
  • Yard wet spots
  • Recurring main clogs
