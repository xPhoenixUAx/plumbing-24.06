How cookies, pixels, browser storage, and similar technologies may be used.

This Cookie Policy explains how FlowCore Plumbing may use cookies and similar technologies on this website. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy, which explains how information may be collected, used, and shared when you use our provider matching website.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your browser or device. Similar technologies may include pixels, tags, scripts, local storage, session storage, software development kits, device identifiers, and analytics identifiers. These tools can help a website function, remember settings, understand usage, measure marketing performance, and support communication or referral workflows.

Why We Use Cookies

We may use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Operate the website, forms, search, page transitions, and interactive request tools.
  • Remember basic preferences or session state.
  • Understand how visitors use pages, service categories, and contact forms.
  • Measure marketing performance when approved analytics or advertising tools are configured.
  • Improve website layout, content, speed, troubleshooting, and conversion paths.
  • Help protect the website from misuse, spam, security issues, or technical errors.

Essential Cookies

Essential cookies and storage are required for core website functionality. They may support navigation, form behavior, page loading, security, session handling, accessibility, and basic interactive features. Disabling essential technologies may cause parts of the website to stop working correctly.

Preference Cookies

Preference cookies may remember settings or choices, such as prior interactions, local browser state, or interface preferences. These help make the website easier to use on return visits.

Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors use the website. For example, they may report page views, traffic sources, clicked links, device types, approximate location, session duration, and form interaction patterns. This helps us improve service category content, request routing, page performance, and overall user experience.

Advertising and Measurement Cookies

Advertising cookies, pixels, or tags may be used to measure campaign performance, understand which pages lead to inquiries, prevent repetitive advertising, or support marketing optimization. These tools may be placed by advertising platforms if they are enabled for the website. We do not hardcode advertising or analytics IDs unless they are provided for the project.

Communication and Referral Tools

Because this website may operate as a lead generation and referral platform, some technologies may support call tracking, form routing, CRM workflows, email follow-up, SMS communication, provider matching, or inquiry attribution. These tools help us understand where a request came from and how to respond to it.

Third-Party Cookies

Third parties may place cookies or similar technologies when their services are used on the website. These may include hosting providers, analytics platforms, advertising networks, call tracking providers, form processors, CRM systems, security tools, or communication services. Third-party cookies are governed by the privacy and cookie practices of those third parties.

Information Collected Through Cookies

Cookies and similar technologies may collect or process information such as IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, referral source, pages viewed, links clicked, session activity, general location, identifiers, and interaction data. If you submit a form, technical data may be associated with your inquiry for routing, analytics, communication, or recordkeeping purposes.

Your Choices

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block cookies, delete cookies, receive warnings before cookies are stored, or limit tracking technologies. Browser controls vary by provider and device.

You may also be able to control certain analytics or advertising technologies through platform-specific opt-out tools, device privacy settings, or industry opt-out resources. If a cookie consent tool is available on the website, you can use it to adjust supported preferences.

What Happens If Cookies Are Disabled?

If you block or delete cookies, the core website content should remain readable, but some features may work differently. Forms, search suggestions, request previews, page transitions, saved preferences, analytics measurement, and communication attribution may be limited or unavailable.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer "Do Not Track" signals. There is no uniform industry standard for responding to those signals. We may respond to cookie preferences through browser settings, consent tools, or platform-specific controls where applicable.

Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically to reflect changes in website functionality, analytics, advertising tools, legal requirements, or business operations. Updates will be posted on this page with the effective date shown above.
